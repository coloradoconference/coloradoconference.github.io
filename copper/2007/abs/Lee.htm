Adaptive and Implicit Immersed Boundary Method with Advection-Electrodiffusion

Pilhwa Lee

251 Mercer Street, New York, NY 10012, USA

Boyce E. Griffith
Charles S. Peskin


Abstract

The Immersed boundary method is a mathematical and computational framework for problems involving the interaction of fluid and structure. We previously proposed an implicit numerical scheme for the advection-electrodiffusion of solutes in the solute-fluid-structure interaction in the paper[1]. Stiff elasticity on the boundary makes the fluid velocity gradient large across the boundary. Chemical potential gradients and electrical potential gradient are also huge across the boundary. Near the immersed boundary, local mesh refinement is needed. In this paper, the Stokes equations are solved adaptively based on the uniform grid approach of the paper[2]. The projection operator is carefully devised by a cell-centered approximate projection method. The advection-electrodiffusion equations are solved by the combination of geometric and algebraic multigrid methods. SAMRAI and Hypre frameworks are the building blocks for the numerical implementation. The adaptive results give us the same accuracy with those on uniform grid with the finest resolution. The implicit scheme make the time stepping flexible within the constraint of CFL.

[1] P. Lee, Charles S. Peskin, Immersed Boundary Method with Advection-Electrodiffusion, (preprint)
[2] Mori, Y., Peskin, C.S., Implicit second order immersed boundary method with boundary mass, (preprint)
