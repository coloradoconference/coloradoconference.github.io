Irad Yavneh
Cyclic Reduction Multigrid Revisited

Department of Computer Science
Technion - Israel Institute of Technology
Haifa 32000
Israel
irad@cs.technion.ac.il
Chen Greif
University of British Columbia, Canada

We revisit an early 90's technique due to Golub and Tuminaro, originally suggested for two-dimensional convection-diffusion. The idea is to perform a single cyclic reduction step before reverting to multigrid. We generalize this idea and introduce a novel smoothing analysis for the resulting problem, which yields explicit analytical results. In particular, we prove for 2+1 point "star" stencils, where is the dimension, that the suitably defined -ellipticity measure is always increased by the cyclic reduction step for symmetric -matrices. Additionally, we introduce a novel relaxation method employing a cyclic reduction step, dubbed cyclic reduction relaxation (CRR), and analyze its smoothing properties, which turn out to be exceptional. Numerical computations show a close agreement between the smoothing analysis results and actual V-cycle convergence factors.
