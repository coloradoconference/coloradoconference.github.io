Smoothed Aggregation Multigrid on Multicore Architectures

Multicore computer architectures present new challenges to linear solvers, including algebraic multigrid multigrid. The advent of many cores has effectively increased the imbalance between node compute power and off-node communication throughput. In this talk we report on our experiences using nonstandard smoothed aggregation multigrid on multicore architectures to address the computation/communication imbalance. Specifically, we consider the case of multigrid with domain decomposition smoothing, where the domains are allowed to cover more than one core in a compute node. We'll present numerical results on thousands of processors.
