Applying smoothed aggregation multigrid (SA) to solve nonsymmetric linear systems, , can be problematic due to a lack of minimization principle in the coarse-grid corrections. We propose an approach that is based on approximately applying SA to the symmetric positive definite matrices or . These matrices, however, are typically full and difficult to compute, and it is therefore not computationally efficient to use these matrices directly to form a coarse-grid correction. Our proposed approach approximates these coarse-grid corrections by using smoothed aggregation to accurately approximate the right and left singular vectors of that correspond to the lowest singular value. These are used to construct the interpolation and restriction operators, respectively. We present some preliminary two-level convergence theory and numerical results.
